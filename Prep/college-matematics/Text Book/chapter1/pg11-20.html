Module 01 / Pages 11-13

Performing Calculations Using PEMDAS

Mathematical grouping structures must be processed systematically to ensure consistent absolute calculation results. PEMDAS rules mandate processing Parentheses first, followed by Exponents/Radicals, left-to-right Multiplication/Division, and finally left-to-right Addition/Subtraction.

Example 6(a)

Evaluating Exponential & Parentheses Groups

(3 * 2)² - 4(6 + 2)

Step 1 (Parentheses): Simplify inside both pairs ➜ (6)² - 4(8)

Step 2 (Exponents): Square the base 6 ➜ 36 - 4(8)

Step 3 (Multiplication): Multiply 4 by 8 ➜ 36 - 32

Final Answer: 4

Example 6(b)

Fractions & Radicals as Grouping Symbols

[(5² - 4) / 7] - √(11 - 2)

Step 1 (Radical Group): Treat the radical floor as a parenthesis group: 11 - 2 = 9 ➜ [(5² - 4) / 7] - √9

Step 2 (Simplify Exponent & Root): Evaluate 5² and √9 ➜ [(25 - 4) / 7] - 3

Step 3 (Fraction Numerator Group): Treat fraction bars as grouping blocks: 25 - 4 = 21 ➜ (21 / 7) - 3

Step 4 (Division): Divide 21 by 7 ➜ 3 - 3

Final Answer: 0

Module 02 / Pages 14-16

Properties Matrix

Properties of addition and multiplication enable systematic rearrangements of algebraic strings. Subtraction and division do not share these properties.

Property Addition Rule Multiplication Rule
Commutative a + b = b + a a * b = b * a
Associative a + (b + c) = (a + b) + c a(bc) = (ab)c
Distributive a * (b + c) = a * b + a * c
Identity a + 0 = a a * 1 = a
Inverse a + (-a) = 0 a * (1/a) = 1 (a ≠ 0)
Example 7(d)

Simplifying with Associative & Inverse Rules

(4/7) * [(2/3) * (7/4)]

Step 1 (Commutative): Swap terms within the parenthesis ➜ (4/7) * [(7/4) * (2/3)]

Step 2 (Associative): Re-group matching base factors ➜ [(4/7) * (7/4)] * (2/3)

Step 3 (Inverse): Reciprocal products collapse to identity ➜ 1 * (2/3)

Final Answer (Identity): 2/3

Module 03 / Pages 17-18

Evaluating & Simplifying Expressions

Substituting specified real numbers into active variable slots lets you isolate and resolve algebraic strings cleanly.

Example 8

Evaluating a Multi-Variable Expression

2x + 3y   [for x = 4, y = -2]

Step 1 (Substitution): Plug values inside respective slots ➜ 2(4) + 3(-2)

Step 2 (Multiplication): Process products left-to-right ➜ 8 + (-6)

Final Answer: 2

Example 9

Simplifying by Combining Like Terms

(4t - 5/4s) - (2/3t + 2s)

Step 1 (Distribution): Distribute the negative sign to remove parentheses ➜ 4t - 5/4s - 2/3t - 2s

Step 2 (Group Like Terms): Rearrange variables ➜ (4t - 2/3t) + (-5/4s - 2s)

Step 3 (Common Denominators): Convert coefficients to matching fractions ➜ (12/3t - 2/3t) + (-5/4s - 8/4s)

Final Answer: 10/3t - 13/4s

Module 04 / Pages 19-20

Exponents & The Zero Exponent Rule

Exponential notation indicates that a base value is multiplied by itself $n$ times. Key operations include the Product, Quotient, and Zero Exponent rules.

Product Rule
aⁿ * aᵐ = aⁿ⁺ᵐ
Quotient Rule
aⁿ / aᵐ = aⁿ⁻ᵐ
Zero Exponent Rule
a⁰ = 1  (a ≠ 0)
Advanced Quotient Rule

Simplifying Complex Polynomial Quotients

5(rs²)² / (rs²)²

Step 1 (Quotient Rule): Subtract the identical denominator power from the numerator power ➜ 5 * (rs²)^(2 - 2)

Step 2 (Zero Power Rule): The matching algebraic component cancels to 1 ➜ 5 * (rs²)⁰ = 5 * 1

Final Answer: 5
